# Wow Moment

The first success condition is emotional, not technical.

## Target feeling

The user records their own beatbox sounds, hears BBXAI arrange them, and thinks:

> Wow. I can actually do this.

That feeling is the acceptance test.

## Why this matters

With AI coding, minimum viable product can accidentally become the final target.

BBXAI should avoid that trap.

A small version is only successful if it proves the feeling. A checklist-complete demo that feels lifeless is not a success.

## What the first version must prove

The first usable version should prove:

- my sounds can become music
- the AI did not replace me
- I am not overwhelmed by production tools
- I can hear a real structure forming
- I want to record another sound and try again

## Bad success metric

> The app has recording, labels, arrangement, and export.

That might be useful, but it is not enough.

## Good success metric

> I heard my own beatbox sounds become something that felt like a real track.

## Design rule

Every early feature should be judged by whether it moves the user closer to that moment.
